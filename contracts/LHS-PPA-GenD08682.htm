2. Lease.

2.1 Lease of Leased Premises. CLIENT hereby demises and leases to OWNER and OWNER hereby demises and leases from CLIENT the Leased Premises. CLIENT hereby represents and warrants that it is the sole and exclusive owner of the Site and is authorized to enter into this Agreement and grant the lease set forth in this Section 2.

2.2 Lease Consideration. The consideration for the lease and use of the Leased Premises by OWNER shall be the construction, ownership and ongoing operation of a dedicated, on-site System by OWNER to provide solar electricity to CLIENT. The lease includes the right to install, maintain, repair and replace solar panels, racking, inverters, solar meters, electric connections, interconnection facilities, control systems, piping and other equipment to permit OWNER to connect the System to CLIENT’s electrical system at the specified delivery point, and reasonable access to CLIENT’s electrical service panels and related equipment. The Parties acknowledge that a Memorandum of Lease in the form attached hereto as Exhibit G, shall be recorded in the official record of the county in which the Leased Premises is located.

2.3 Term of the Lease. The term of this lease shall be coterminous with the Term. In the event OWNER shall hold over after the Term, such holdover shall be a tenancy only from month to month and shall be governed by the terms, covenants and conditions contained in this Agreement.

2.4 Access to Site. CLIENT shall provide OWNER with access to the Site and Leased Premises as reasonably necessary to allow OWNER to perform the Installation Work and maintain the System, including ingress and egress rights to the Site for OWNER and its employees, agents, owners and utility personnel and access to electrical panels and conduits to interconnect the System with the Site electrical wiring. OWNER may use the Site as much as reasonably necessary for the temporary storage and staging of tools, materials, and equipment and for the parking of construction crew vehicles and temporary construction trailers and facilities reasonably necessary during the Installation Work, removal work and access for rigging and material handling. After completion of construction, any additional areas shall be restored to their previous condition by OWNER. CLIENT and its authorized representatives shall at all times have access to and the right to observe the Installation Work, subject to compliance with OWNER’s safety rules, but shall not interfere with the Installation Work or handle any OWNER equipment or the System without written authorization from OWNER. OWNER will not unreasonably interfere with the operation of CLIENT’s business and will not unnecessarily handle any CLIENT equipment without written authorization from CLIENT.

2.5 Ownership. The System and all alterations, additions, improvements or installations made to it by OWNER and all OWNER property used in connection with the installation, operation and maintenance of the System is, and shall remain, the personal property of OWNER. In no event shall the System be deemed to be a fixture, nor shall CLIENT, nor anyone claiming by, through, or under CLIENT, including but not limited to any present or future mortgagee of the Site or the Leased Premises, have any rights in or to the System at any time except as otherwise provided herein. OWNER may grant or cause to be granted to a third party a security interest in the System or the Leased Premises, and CLIENT expressly disclaims and waives any rights it may have in the System and the Leased Premises (to the extent inconsistent with this Agreement), at any time and from time to time, pursuant to the Agreement, at law or in equity.

5.3 Malfunctions and Emergencies.

5.3.1 Notice. Each Party shall notify the other Party within twenty-four (24) hours following the discovery by it of (a) any material malfunction in the operation of the System or (b) an interruption in the supply of Solar Services. OWNER and CLIENT shall each designate personnel (currently the personnel specified as Contacts in Section 1) and establish procedures to notify each other of such conditions requiring OWNER’s repair or alteration at all times, twenty-four (24) hours per day, including weekends and holidays. OWNER and CLIENT each shall notify the other Party immediately upon the discovery of an emergency condition in the System.

5.3.2 Repairs. OWNER shall commence repairs to the malfunctioning System and restore the supply of Energy as soon as reasonably possible after notice or upon its own discovery of any of the conditions specified in Section 5.3.1 during normal business hours and, subject to Section 5.3, take steps to mobilize personnel to commence repairs after notice or discovery of a condition requiring repair or other corrective action. If an emergency condition exists, OWNER shall dispatch the appropriate personnel immediately upon becoming aware thereof to perform the necessary repairs or corrective action in an expeditious and safe manner. For routine and emergency repairs, the Parties shall contact the persons identified as Contacts in Section 1.

5.3.3 Reimbursement. To the extent any malfunction, interruption or other problem with the System is caused by CLIENT or a third party, CLIENT shall reimburse OWNER for the cost of providing such repair and other services on a time and materials basis at OWNER’s then standard rates.

5.4 Metering

5.4.1 Maintenance and Testing. OWNER shall install and maintain a utility-grade kilowatt-hour (“kWh”) meter (“Meter”) for the measurement of Energy provided to CLIENT. OWNER shall annually furnish a copy of all technical specifications and accuracy calibrations for the Meter. OWNER shall test the Meter biannually.

5.4.2 Adjustments. OWNER shall be responsible for costs associated with testing the Meter. If testing of a Meter pursuant to Section 5.4.1 indicates that such Meter is in error by more than two percent (2%), then OWNER shall promptly repair or replace such Meter, OWNER shall make a corresponding adjustment to the records of the amount of Energy based on such test results for (a) the actual period of time when such error caused inaccurate meter recordings, if such period can be determined to the mutual satisfaction of the Parties, or (b) if such period cannot be so determined, then a period equal to one-half (1/2) of the period from the later of (i) the date of the last previous test confirming accurate metering and (ii) the date the Meter was placed into service; provided, however, that such period shall in no case exceed two (2) years.

5.5 Curtailment Outages. CLIENT shall be permitted to curtail receipt of Energy from the System between the hours of 6pm and 9am for a maximum of twenty-four (24) hours per calendar year during the Term, during which periods CLIENT shall not be obligated to accept or pay for Energy; provided, however, that CLIENT shall have notified OWNER in writing of the date, time and duration of each such curtailment (“Scheduled Outage”) at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance of the commencement of such Scheduled Outage. In the event that Scheduled Outages at the Site exceed twenty-four (24) hours in total per calendar year for a reason other than a Force Majeure, OWNER shall reasonably estimate the amount of Energy that would have been delivered to CLIENT during each hour of such excess and shall invoice CLIENT for such amount (which shall be payable in accordance with Section 7.3) as well as amounts necessary to cover the value of lost Environmental Benefits associated with the generation and sale of such Energy.

Upon the exercise of the foregoing purchase option plus receipt of the greater of the Buyout Value and the Fair Market Value and all other amounts then owing by CLIENT to OWNER, the Parties will execute all documents necessary to cause title to the System to pass to CLIENT as-is, where-is.

11.4.3 upon the elimination or alteration of one or more Environmental Financial Incentives or other change in law that results in a material adverse economic impact on OWNER.
